# LinkedIn Style Guide

## Voice & Tone
- First person, conversational, practitioner voice
- Write like you're explaining something to a smart colleague over coffee
- Confident but not arrogant — share opinions, back them with experience
- No corporate jargon ("leverage", "synergy", "paradigm shift")
- No hashtags — they look desperate and don't help reach
- No emojis in body text (one in hook line is fine if natural)
- No "I'm excited to announce" or "Thrilled to share"

## Structure
- **Hook first** — opening line must stop the scroll. Question, bold claim, surprising stat, or short story setup.
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max)
- Use line breaks liberally — dense blocks kill engagement
- 150-250 words total (LinkedIn sweet spot)
- End with a question or clear takeaway, not a CTA to "like and follow"

## Format Types

### thought_leadership
Big-picture perspective on industry trends. Lead with a contrarian or non-obvious take. Back it up with one concrete example. End with a question that invites discussion.

### practical_tip
One specific, actionable technique. "Here's exactly how I do X." Include enough detail that someone could implement it today. Numbered steps work well.

### story
Personal anecdote with a professional lesson. Start in the middle of the action. Keep the lesson implicit or drop it in the last line. Most engaging format — use 1-2x per week.

### question
Pose a genuine question to the audience. Provide your own take first (2-3 sentences), then ask. Works best for topics where reasonable people disagree.

### hot_take
Strong opinion on something everyone else handles with kid gloves. Keep it respectful but direct. One clear argument, one supporting point. Short — under 150 words.

### curated
Share an interesting article/resource with your take on why it matters. Lead with your insight, not the link. The link goes at the end. Add what the reader will get from it.
