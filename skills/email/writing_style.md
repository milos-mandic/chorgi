# Milos's Email Writing Style

## Voice & Tone
- Concise, warm but professional
- No fluff or filler — gets to the point quickly
- First-person, natural tone (not corporate-speak)
- Confident but not pushy

## Structure
- Opens with a personal touch (references shared experience, meeting, event)
- Short paragraphs, generous line breaks for readability
- Clear ask or CTA when applicable
- Signs off with "Best," or "Cheers," followed by "Milos"

## What to Avoid
- Overly formal language ("I hope this email finds you well", "per our discussion")
- Long-winded explanations
- Bullet points in email body (use prose)
- Exclamation marks overuse (one max per email)
- Filler phrases ("Just wanted to reach out", "I wanted to follow up")

## Example Emails

### Example 1 — Post-event follow-up (simple)
```
Hey Malik,

Really enjoyed our conversation at the Drawbridge dinner. One of the highlights of the trip for me.

Would love to stay in touch and continue the conversation at some point. No agenda, just think there's a lot of overlap in how we think about this space.

Best,
Milos
```

### Example 2 — Post-event with specific ask
```
Hey Rahul,

Great meeting you at the FDE conference.

As Priya mentioned during my intro, I'm also running FDE Hub (fdehub.org) and as part of it, doing Q&A interviews with practitioners and leaders from different companies.

I would love to do a session with you. The conversations are relaxed, around 45 minutes, and I send general topics in advance, but I also allow the conversation to flow.

Here are a couple of recent ones so you can get a feel for the format:
https://www.fdehub.org/p/startup-within-a-startup-a-conversation (Rory O'Brien, HappyRobot)
https://www.fdehub.org/p/what-kind-of-fde-are-you-a-conversation (Vlad Shulman, Baseten)

Would you be up for it?

Best,
Milos
```

```

```
